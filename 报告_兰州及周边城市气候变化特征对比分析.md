# 兰州及周边城市气候变化特征对比分析（1960–2025）

> **Author:** Vitality-Johnny | **Institution:** Lanzhou University · Atmospheric Sciences  
> **Data:** Open-Meteo ERA5 (unified grid reanalysis) | **Period:** 1960–2025  
> **Methods:** Linear regression · Mann-Kendall test · Morlet wavelet · Extreme climate indices

---

## Abstract

This study analyzes monthly temperature and precipitation trends for **Lanzhou, Xi'an, and Xining** over **66 years (1960–2025)** using **unified ERA5 reanalysis data** from Open-Meteo. All three cities exhibit statistically significant warming (p<0.001): Lanzhou +0.034°C/yr, Xi'an +0.030°C/yr, Xining +0.038°C/yr. Precipitation shows a consistent drying trend across all cities (−2.4 to −6.2 mm/yr). Mann-Kendall mutation tests identify the late 1980s/early 1990s as a key climate shift period. Morlet wavelet analysis reveals a dominant ~4-year ENSO-related cycle in Lanzhou and Xi'an, while Xining exhibits a longer ~8-year periodicity. Extreme cold months are declining while warm months show no significant trend.

**Keywords:** climate change, Northwest China, ERA5, Mann-Kendall, wavelet analysis

---

## 1. Introduction

Understanding regional climate change in arid and semi-arid Northwest China is critical. Lanzhou, Xi'an, and Xining form a northwest-southeast transect with similar latitudes but contrasting elevations (400–2260 m), providing a natural laboratory for comparing climate responses.

| City | Lat | Lon | Elevation | Climate |
|------|-----|-----|-----------|---------|
| Lanzhou | 36.05°N | 103.88°E | ~1520m | Semi-arid temperate |
| Xi'an | 34.30°N | 108.93°E | ~400m | Warm temperate semi-humid |
| Xining | 36.62°N | 101.77°E | ~2260m | Plateau semi-arid |

**Key design decision:** All data comes from a **single unified source** (ERA5 via Open-Meteo) to avoid the systematic biases introduced by mixing station observations with reanalysis products. An earlier version of this project using mixed sources (GHCN + lishi.tianqi + Open-Meteo) produced a spurious "Lanzhou cooling" signal — a lesson in the importance of data source consistency.

## 2. Data & Methods

### 2.1 Data

| Source | Variables | Resolution | Coverage |
|--------|-----------|------------|----------|
| **Open-Meteo Archive** (ERA5) | Daily T₂ₘ mean, precipitation sum | 0.25° grid | 1960-01-01 → 2025-12-31 |

Daily values are aggregated to monthly means (temperature) and monthly sums (precipitation). All output is in UTF-8 BOM CSV format (wide: `year, Jan, Feb, ..., Dec`).

### 2.2 Methods

- **Linear regression** with R² and p-values
- **Mann-Kendall trend test** (non-parametric, α=0.05)
- **Mann-Kendall mutation test** (UF/UB curves, intersection = change point)
- **Morlet continuous wavelet transform** (CWT) for periodicity detection
- **Extreme climate indices:** warm/cold months (>90th/<10th percentile), wet months (>95th percentile), dry months (<5mm)

**Tools:** Python 3.12 · pandas · matplotlib · scipy · requests

| City | 66yr Mean | Trend | R² | p-value | ΔT (66yr) |
|------|-----------|-------|-----|---------|-----------|
| Lanzhou | 10.1°C | **+0.034°C/yr** | 0.622 | <0.001 | +2.2°C |
| Xi'an | 13.9°C | **+0.030°C/yr** | 0.554 | <0.001 | +2.0°C |

All three cities are warming at highly significant rates (p<0.001). The warming is robust — R² values of 0.55–0.65 indicate a clear linear trend over 66 years. Xining shows the fastest warming rate (+0.038°C/yr), consistent with the well-documented elevation-dependent warming phenomenon on the Tibetan Plateau.

### 3.2 Precipitation Trends

![Fig2](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/Vitality-Johnny/lanzhou-climate-analysis/main/fig2_%E5%B9%B4%E9%99%8D%E6%B0%B4%E8%B6%8B%E5%8A%BF.png)

| City | 66yr Mean | Trend | R² | p-value |
|------|-----------|-------|-----|---------|
| Lanzhou | 457 mm | **−2.4 mm/yr** | 0.222 | <0.001 |
| Xi'an | 888 mm | **−6.2 mm/yr** | 0.329 | <0.001 |
| Xining | 717 mm | **−4.1 mm/yr** | 0.398 | <0.001 |

A consistent drying trend across all three cities. Xi'an shows the largest absolute decline (−6.2 mm/yr, ~410 mm over 66 years), though with the weakest R² due to high interannual variability (CV=22%). Xining's precipitation decline has the strongest R² (0.40), indicating a relatively steady drying signal.

### 3.5 Extended Analysis

#### 3.5.1 MK Mutation Test

![ExtA](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/Vitality-Johnny/lanzhou-climate-analysis/main/ext_figA_MK%E7%AA%81%E5%8F%98%E6%A3%80%E9%AA%8C.png)

The UF/UB curves identify the **late 1980s to early 1990s** as a critical climate shift period for all three cities, corresponding to the well-known "abrupt warming" observed across China around 1987–1993. Temperature UF curves cross above zero and exceed the +1.96 significance threshold during this period.

#### 3.5.2 Wavelet Periodicity

- **Lanzhou & Xi'an:** Dominant ~4-year cycle in temperature → characteristic of ENSO modulation

## 4. Conclusions

1. **Robust warming:** All three cities warmed at ~0.03–0.04°C/yr over 1960–2025 (p<0.001), totaling +2.0–2.5°C. This is consistent with global and regional warming patterns.

2. **Consistent drying:** Annual precipitation declined by 2.4–6.2 mm/yr across all cities, with Xining showing the steadiest trend (R²=0.40).

3. **Climate shift in late 1980s:** MK mutation analysis pinpoints the 1987–1993 period as a key transition, aligning with documented climate regime shifts in China.

4. **Methodological lesson:** An earlier iteration using mixed data sources (GHCN + lishi.tianqi + Open-Meteo) falsely suggested Lanzhou was cooling. Switching to a single unified source (ERA5) revealed the true warming signal. **Data source consistency is non-negotiable for multi-site climate comparisons.**

### 4.1 Limitations

- **Reanalysis, not observations:** ERA5 is a model-assimilated product. Station-level validation would strengthen conclusions.
- **Grid resolution:** 0.25° (~28 km) may miss fine-scale urban effects.
- **Precipitation uncertainty:** Reanalysis precipitation is less reliable than temperature, especially in complex terrain like the Tibetan Plateau margin.
